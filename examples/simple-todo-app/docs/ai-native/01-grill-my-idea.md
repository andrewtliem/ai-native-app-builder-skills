# Grill My Idea — Simple Todo App

## Sources Read
- Student raw idea: “I want to build a todo app for my class tasks.”

## Original Idea
A simple todo app where students can add tasks for their classes and mark them done.

## Pressure Test

### What problem is this solving?
Students forget homework, reading, project milestones, and quiz preparation because tasks are scattered across chats, LMS pages, and notebooks.

### Who is the user?
A university student managing tasks across multiple courses.

### Why might this be too generic?
Todo apps are common. The student must make the scope specific enough for learning: course-based tasks, due dates, and simple status tracking.

### Assumptions
- The student wants a beginner-friendly project.
- The app should work without a backend for the first version.
- Data can be stored locally in the browser.

### Risks
- The app becomes a clone with no clear learning goal.
- The student adds login, collaboration, calendar sync, and notifications too early.
- The student trusts AI-generated UI code without checking accessibility.

## Better Framing
Build a local-first course task tracker for students that lets them add, filter, complete, and review course tasks without creating an account.

## Decision
Proceed, but keep v1 small and testable.
